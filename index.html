What Is Medical Acupuncture?

A clinician-guided treatment using very fine needles at specific points to help reduce pain and calm the nervous system.

How a session works

  1. Clinical assessment and a tailored plan (needle number and placement vary).
  2. Needle insertion; may include gentle manual stimulation or low-frequency electrical currents.
  3. Short rest, followed by review and next steps.

How it works

Stimulating nerves in skin and muscle can prompt release of natural pain-relievers (e.g., endorphins, serotonin) and influence how pain signals are processed in the brain and spinal cord.

Benefits & Evidence

Clinical research supports short-term relief for lower-back pain, knee pain, neck pain, and certain headaches (migraines, tension-type). It has also been shown to help reduce nausea in specific situations. Evidence varies by condition, and improvement isn't guaranteed.

  • Pain management: back, knee, neck
  • Headaches: migraine, tension-type
  • Nausea: selected contexts (e.g., some postoperative/therapy settings)

Why Choose a Medical Acupuncturist?

You'll work with a healthcare professional trained in both conventional medicine and acupuncture. They can interpret your history, tests, and medications, and coordinate with your GP if needed.

Acupuncture can relieve symptoms and improve quality of life, but it is not a substitute for treatment of serious underlying conditions. It should be used alongside, not instead of, standard medical care.

A Blend of Tradition and Modern Science

Acupuncture has ancient roots in China (possibly 4,000+ years). Interest in the West grew in the 20th century. Research has clarified mechanisms and challenged myths, supporting its use alongside modern treatments.

Today, acupuncture is integrated into many UK pain services and offered by a growing number of doctors and specialists.
